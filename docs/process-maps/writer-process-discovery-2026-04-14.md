# Writer Process Discovery — Interview Prep

**Date:** 2026-04-14
**Participants:** Senior Writers, Writer(s), Paulo
**Goal:** Map the as-is content production workflow from the writer/senior writer perspective

---

## Interview Approach

Two sessions:
1. **Senior writers + Paulo** — map the high-level process spine
2. **Writer(s)** — add nuance, day-to-day specifics, where their process diverges

Core principle: ask about their life, not our idea. Past tense, specific instances. Listen 80%, talk 20%.

---

## Interview Flow (30-40 min)

### 1. Warm-Up (2 min)

> "Thanks for doing this. No wrong answers — I just want to understand how work actually flows through your hands day to day."

- Set expectations: learning session, not evaluation
- Ask permission to record

### 2. Anchor to a Specific Project (20 min)

> "Pick a video you finished recently — walk me through it from the moment you first heard about it to the moment it was done. Every step, every app, every handoff."

This forces concrete details instead of "well, usually I..."

**Probe at every handoff point** — when they say "then I send it to [person]":
- "How do you send it? Slack? Notion? Email?"
- "How do they know it's ready?"
- "What happens if they're not available?"
- "Has anything ever fallen through the cracks here?"

**Listen for (don't ask directly — probe when they come up):**
- Which Notion pages/databases they actually open daily
- Where they check project status
- How they know what to work on next
- What they do when they're blocked/waiting
- Where files live (Dropbox folders, Google Docs, etc.)
- Any workarounds or "I just DM them on Slack" patterns
- Who they communicate with most and how

### 3. Tools Inventory (5 min)

> "Can you list every app, page, or tool you touched for that project?"

Get the full stack: Notion, Slack, Dropbox, Google Docs, Frame.io, etc.

### 4. Pain Points (5 min)

> "What's the hardest part of your workflow? Where do things break or slow down?"

Follow up with:
- "Can you give me a specific example of when that happened?"
- "How often does that happen?"
- "What do you do when it happens?"

### 5. Magic Wand (3 min)

> "If you could change one thing about how work flows through your hands, what would it be?"

### 6. Catch-All (2 min)

> "Anything I didn't ask that you think matters?"
> "Who else should I talk to about this?"

---

## For the Writer Session (add-on)

Same structure, but open with:

> "We just talked with the senior writers. Where does your process differ from theirs?"

This lets them build on the first session instead of repeating it. Also probe:
- How do they receive assignments from senior writers?
- What does "done" look like from their side vs. what gets handed back?
- Do they ever get conflicting instructions from different people?

---

## App Demo Questions (after the interview)

Show them the My Board (Kanban) and ask:

**Volume & workload:**
- "How many projects did you have going last month? Roughly."
- "How many are you actively working on right now, at this moment?"
- "What's the most you've ever juggled at once? Did anything slip?"

**Board usefulness:**
- "If you opened this board Monday morning, does it show you what to work on first?"
- "What info do you need to see on a card without clicking into it?"
- "Are these status columns the right buckets for how work actually moves through your hands?"
- "Is anything missing — a status or stage that you go through that isn't represented here?"

**Priority & urgency:**
- "How do you know which project is most urgent right now? Is there a signal, or do you just know?"
- "Do you ever get surprised by a deadline — something you didn't know was due?"

**Collaboration:**
- "When you look at a project, do you need to see who else is working on it?"
- "Would it help to see what the editor or strategist is doing, or do you only care about your part?"
